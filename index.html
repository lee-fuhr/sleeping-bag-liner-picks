How we choose our picks

We don’t claim to be lab testers or take every liner out for a month-long thru-hike. Instead, we lean on what actually matters: long-term owner feedback from forums, expert reviews from trusted outdoor publications, and our own conversations with gear repair shops about what fails first. Over time, patterns emerge, zippers that jam, fabrics that pill, seams that split after a season. We pay attention to those patterns. We also look at how well each liner performs across different real-world conditions: does it still breathe when you’re sleeping on a humid night? Does it slide around inside the bag? Can you easily wash it without losing shape? We prioritize liners that are consistently recommended by people who own them, not just by marketing copy. If a product has a reputation for falling apart or not doing what it promises, it doesn’t make our list, no matter how much it’s advertised.
