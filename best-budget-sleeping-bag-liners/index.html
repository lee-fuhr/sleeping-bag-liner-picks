A sleeping bag liner does two smart things: it adds a warm layer inside your bag (boosting its temperature rating by 10–15°F) and protects the bag’s interior from sweat and dirt, so you wash the liner instead of the whole bag. For budget-conscious campers, a liner is an easy upgrade that extends your gear’s life without a big investment. Choosing the right budget liner comes down to three things: material (fleece for warmth, silk or polyester for packability), weight (lighter is better for backpacking), and size (some liners are snug, others roomy). Look for a shape that matches your sleeping bag, rectangular liners fit most bags, while mummy-style liners are more efficient for heat retention.
